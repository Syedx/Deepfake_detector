Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about deepfakes and our detection system

What are deepfakes?

Deepfakes are synthetic media in which a person's likeness is replaced with someone else's using artificial intelligence techniques, typically deep learning. The term refers to both the technology and the resulting media. Deepfakes can be photos, videos, or audio recordings that appear authentic but are actually manipulated or completely generated by AI.

The technology has become increasingly sophisticated and accessible, raising concerns about potential misuse for misinformation, fraud, or impersonation.

How accurate is your deepfake detection system?

Our deepfake detection system achieves over 95% accuracy on benchmark datasets. However, it's important to understand that as deepfake technology evolves, detection becomes more challenging.

The system is most effective against current-generation deepfakes, and we continuously update our models to keep pace with advancements in manipulation techniques. For each analysis, we provide a confidence score to help you understand the reliability of our classification.

What file types and sizes do you support?

For images, we support:

  • JPEG/JPG
  • PNG
  • BMP

For videos, we support:

  • MP4
  • AVI
  • MOV

The maximum file size is 50MB for both images and videos. For optimal performance, we recommend images with a resolution of at least 512x512 pixels.

How does your system detect deepfakes?

Our system uses a multi-faceted approach to detect manipulated media:

  1. Visual Analysis: Our convolutional neural network (CNN) analyzes pixel-level patterns and inconsistencies that are typical in synthetic media but often invisible to the human eye.
  2. Metadata Analysis: We examine EXIF data and other metadata for signs of editing or inconsistencies.
  3. Facial Analysis: For images containing faces, we perform specialized detection focused on artifacts common in facial manipulations.

The combined approach provides a more robust detection system than relying on any single method alone.

What is a heatmap and how do I interpret it?

The heatmap visualization uses a technique called Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) to highlight regions of the image that most influenced the classification decision.

Areas with warmer colors (red, orange, yellow) had a stronger influence on the decision, while cooler colors (blue, green) had less impact. For deepfakes, the highlighted areas often show manipulation artifacts or inconsistencies that the AI detected.

This visualization helps you understand why our system made its classification and where potential manipulations might be located in the image.

Do you store my uploaded images or videos?

We do not permanently store your uploaded media. Files are temporarily stored only for the duration needed to process them and are automatically deleted after 24 hours.

Analysis results (including metadata and detection results) are also temporarily stored to enable you to access them during your session, but they are similarly deleted after 24 hours.

We prioritize user privacy and do not use uploaded content for training our models unless you explicitly opt in to contribute to our research.

Can your system detect AI-generated images (not just face swaps)?

Yes, our system can detect various types of AI-generated or manipulated content, including:

  • Face-swapped deepfakes
  • Fully AI-generated images (e.g., from DALL-E, Midjourney, or Stable Diffusion)
  • Manipulated images with added, removed, or altered elements
  • Style transfer manipulations

However, detection accuracy may vary depending on the specific generation method and quality. Our system performs best on facial manipulations, but continuously improves for all types of synthetic media.

Are there any API or integration options available?

Yes, we offer API access for developers and organizations who want to integrate our deepfake detection capabilities into their own applications or workflows.

Our API provides:

  • RESTful endpoints for image and video analysis
  • JSON responses with detection results, confidence scores, and explanations
  • Optional heatmap visualizations
  • Batch processing capabilities
